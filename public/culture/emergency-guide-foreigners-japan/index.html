Emergency Guide for Foreigners in Japan (Police, Fire, Hospital)

What to do in an emergency in Japan as a foreigner. Emergency numbers, what to say, how to get help in English, and what to do in an earthquake.

Calling 110 (Police)#

When to call:

  • You witness or are a victim of a crime
  • A traffic accident
  • Someone suspicious or threatening

What to say:

“English please. I need police. I am at [location].”

Many dispatch operators have basic English. Stay calm and speak slowly.

Important: Police in Japan are generally safe to contact. You will NOT get in trouble just for calling.

Calling 119 (Fire/Ambulance)#

When to call:

  • Medical emergency
  • Fire
  • Someone unconscious or seriously injured

What to say:

“Ambulance please. [Your address]. I speak English.”

Ambulances in Japan are free. Don’t hesitate to call.

Earthquake Emergency Guide#

Japan has earthquakes regularly. Here’s what to do:

During an earthquake:#

  1. Stay low — get under a desk or table
  2. Protect your head with a bag or cushion
  3. Stay away from windows and falling objects
  4. Do NOT run outside immediately — falling glass and debris are dangerous
  5. If in bed: stay in bed and cover your head with a pillow

After the shaking stops:#

  1. Check for gas leaks — if you smell gas, open windows and leave immediately
  2. Check yourself and others for injuries
  3. Go outside to an open space
  4. Follow instructions from emergency broadcast (NHK World Radio Japan)

Emergency alerts on your phone:#

Your phone will automatically receive J-Alert earthquake warnings in Japanese. The sound is loud and unmistakable. Take it seriously.

Finding Evacuation Sites#

Every neighborhood has a designated evacuation site (避難場所):

  • Usually a park, school, or community center
  • Look for the green evacuation sign: 🔵 (tsunami) or standard evacuation symbol
  • Check your local city hall website for your nearest site

If You’re a Crime Victim#

  1. Call 110 or go to the nearest police box (交番)
  2. You can file a report in English — bring a translation app
  3. Contact your embassy or consulate — they can assist foreigners in legal trouble

Embassy contacts (Tokyo):

  • Philippines: 03-5562-1600
  • Vietnam: 03-3466-3313
  • Indonesia: 03-3441-4201

Lost Your Passport or Residence Card?#

Lost residence card: Report to immigration and apply for a replacement within 14 days. Bring your passport and photo to the nearest immigration office.

Lost passport: Contact your embassy immediately to get an emergency passport.
