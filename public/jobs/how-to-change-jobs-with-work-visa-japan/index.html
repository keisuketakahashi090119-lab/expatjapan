How to Change Jobs with a Work Visa in Japan (2025 Guide)

Can you change jobs on a work visa in Japan? Yes — here's exactly how to do it, what to tell immigration, and how to avoid losing your visa status.

Changing jobs in Japan as a foreigner feels scary. Will you lose your visa? Do you need to tell immigration? Can your new employer sponsor you?

The short answer: yes, you can change jobs — but there are rules you must follow.

Can You Change Jobs on a Work Visa?#

Yes. Most work visas in Japan (like Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services, also called “Gijinkoku”) are not tied to a specific employer.

You are allowed to change companies as long as your new job matches your visa category.

Step 1 — Check Your Visa Category#

Look at your residence card (在留カード). The back shows your visa status.

Visa TypeCan Change Jobs?
Engineer / Specialist (技人国)Yes, if new job matches
Skilled Labor (技能)Yes, same field only
Tokutei Gino (特定技能)Yes, within same industry
Technical Intern Training (技能実習)No — you cannot freely change

Step 2 — Report the Change to Immigration#

Within 14 days of changing jobs, you must report to immigration.

How to report:

  1. Go to immi-moj.go.jp (online) or visit your local immigration office
  2. Submit a “Notification of Change of Contract Organization” (契約機関に関する届出)
  3. You will need: residence card, new company name, address, and your job title

This is free and takes about 10 minutes online.

Step 3 — Confirm Your New Job Matches Your Visa#

This is the most important step. Immigration will check whether your new role fits your visa category when you renew.

Examples:

  • Visa: Engineer → New job: Software developer ✅
  • Visa: Engineer → New job: Restaurant staff ❌

If your new job does not match, you need to apply for a change of status before you start working.

What About the Gap Between Jobs?#

You can stay in Japan while job hunting. You have until your visa expiry date to find a new job.

If you need more time, apply for a designated activities visa (特定活動) which gives you up to 6 months to job hunt.

Best Job Sites for Foreigners in Japan#

  • doda — large database, English support available
  • Daijob — specifically for bilingual and foreign professionals
  • GaijinPot Jobs — popular among expats
  • LinkedIn Japan — good for professional roles

Summary#

  1. Check your visa type allows job changes
  2. Report to immigration within 14 days
  3. Make sure your new job matches your visa category
  4. You have until visa expiry to find a new job

Changing jobs in Japan is very possible. The key is reporting on time and staying in the right job category.
