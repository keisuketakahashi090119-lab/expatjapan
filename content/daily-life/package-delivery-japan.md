Japan's delivery infrastructure is among the best in the world — precise, reliable, and remarkably convenient. But the system has quirks that trip up newcomers.

---

## Major Delivery Companies

| Company | Logo Color | Common For |
|---|---|---|
| **ヤマト運輸 (Yamato / Kuroneko)** | Black cat | Amazon, general parcels |
| **佐川急便 (Sagawa)** | Red | Rakuten, general parcels |
| **日本郵便 (Japan Post)** | Red/White | Letters, government, some Amazon |
| **Amazon Delivery** | Blue | Amazon orders |

Each company has its own tracking system and redelivery process.

---

## What Happens If You Miss a Delivery

This is the most important thing to know. Japan is a **signature-required** delivery culture. Drivers will not leave packages unattended without permission.

When you miss a delivery:
1. A **不在票 (fuzai-hyo)** — missed delivery slip — is left in your mailbox
2. The package goes back to the local delivery center
3. You must **request redelivery** within the holding period (usually 7–14 days)

**Don't ignore the slip** — packages are returned to sender if not picked up.

---

## How to Request Redelivery

### By Phone (Easiest)
- Call the number on the slip
- Automated system available 24/7 (Japanese menu — press numbers)
- Specify your preferred date and time window

### Online
**Yamato (ヤマト):**
- [kuronekoyamato.co.jp](https://www.kuronekoyamato.co.jp/)
- Enter the tracking number from the slip
- Choose redelivery date and time

**Sagawa (佐川):**
- [sagawa-exp.co.jp](https://www.sagawa-exp.co.jp/)
- Same process

**Japan Post:**
- [post.japanpost.jp](https://www.post.japanpost.jp/)
- Can also pick up at any post office

### By QR Code
Many newer slips have a QR code — scan to go directly to the redelivery form.

### Delivery Time Windows
You can choose:
- 午前中 (Gozen-chu) — Morning (before noon)
- 14:00–16:00
- 16:00–18:00
- 18:00–20:00
- 19:00–21:00

## Setting Up a Safe Place (置き配)

Many carriers now allow **置き配 (Okikuhai)** — leaving packages at a designated spot:
- Doorstep
- Mailbox (if large enough)
- Building parcel locker

**Amazon Delivery:** You can set your preferred delivery location in your Amazon Japan account settings. This is the easiest way to avoid missed deliveries.

---

## Parcel Lockers (宅配ボックス)

Many apartment buildings have automated parcel lockers at the entrance. The delivery person places your package inside and leaves a code or notification.

- **Code** is written on the fuzai-hyo or sent to your phone
- Open the locker using the code
- Available 24/7 — no need to coordinate with drivers

If your building doesn't have one, portable parcel locker bags (*takuhai box*) can be purchased for apartment doors.

---

## Amazon Japan Delivery Tips

- **Amazon Lockers (Amazon Hub)** — pick up at stations, convenience stores, or lockers; available for most orders
- **Convenience store pickup** — select "コンビニ・宅配便ロッカー受け取り" at checkout
- **Set delivery instructions** in Japanese in your account to avoid missed deliveries

## Sending Packages

### Domestic (Within Japan)

**ヤマト宅急便 or 佐川急便:**
- Drop off at convenience stores (Lawson → Yamato, 7-Eleven → Yamato, FamilyMart → Yamato)
- Or call for pickup from home
- Cost: ¥500–1,500 depending on size/weight/distance

**Japan Post Yu-Pack (ゆうパック):**
- Available at all post offices
- Flat-rate boxes available (レターパック): ¥370–520 for letters/documents

### International Shipping from Japan

**EMS (国際スピード郵便):** Japan Post's international express service — reliable, trackable, cost-effective.

**Cost examples:**
- 0.5kg to USA: ~¥2,700 EMS
- 2kg to Europe: ~¥5,000 EMS
- Documents: ¥1,400–2,500 depending on zone

**DHL/FedEx/UPS Japan:** Available at major cities; faster but significantly more expensive.

---

## Returning Items

Most Japanese online shopping services (Amazon, Rakuten, Zozotown) provide return labels. Process:
1. Print the return label (or show QR code at convenience store)
2. Drop at the appropriate convenience store or call for pickup
