Taxes in Japan might seem complicated, but for most employed foreigners the process is simple. Here's everything you need to know.

---

## Do Foreigners Have to Pay Tax in Japan?

**Yes.** If you live in Japan and earn income here, you must pay Japanese income tax.

This applies to everyone — regardless of nationality.

---

## Two Types of Tax Residents

Japan divides taxpayers into two categories:

| Type | Who | What's taxed |
|---|---|---|
| **Resident** | Lived in Japan 1+ year | All worldwide income |
| **Non-resident** | Less than 1 year | Japan-source income only |

Most foreigners working in Japan are **residents** for tax purposes.

---

## Do You Need to File a Tax Return?

**Most employed workers: NO — your company handles it.**

If you work for a Japanese company, your employer:
- Deducts income tax from your monthly salary
- Does a **year-end tax adjustment (年末調整)** in December
- Files on your behalf

You receive a **tax withholding slip (源泉徴収票)** in January confirming your taxes for the year.

---

## Who DOES Need to File a Tax Return?

You need to file a **確定申告 (Kakutei Shinkoku)** if:

- You have **two or more jobs**
- You are **self-employed or freelance**
- Your annual income exceeds **¥20 million**
- You changed jobs and have income from multiple companies
- You have **overseas income**
- You want to claim deductions (medical expenses, donations, home loan)

---

## Tax Filing Period

**Filing period: February 16 – March 15 each year**

For the 2024 tax year → file between February 16 – March 15, 2025.

---

## How to File a Tax Return

### Method 1 — e-Tax (Online) — Recommended

1. Get a **My Number Card** with IC chip enabled
2. Go to **e-tax.nta.go.jp**
3. Log in with your My Number Card
4. Fill in your income and deductions
5. Submit online

**Best for:** People with a My Number Card and a computer.

---

### Method 2 — At the Tax Office

1. Go to your nearest **Tax Office (税務署)**
2. Bring: My Number Card or notification, all income documents, bank account details
3. Staff will help you fill in the form
4. Lines are long in February–March — go early

---

## Japan's Income Tax Rates

| Annual Income | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ¥1,950,000 | 5% |
| ¥1,950,001 – ¥3,300,000 | 10% |
| ¥3,300,001 – ¥6,950,000 | 20% |
| ¥6,950,001 – ¥9,000,000 | 23% |
| ¥9,000,001 – ¥18,000,000 | 33% |
| Over ¥18,000,000 | 40–45% |

Plus: **Resident tax (住民税)** of approximately 10% on top.

---

## Resident Tax (住民税)

This is a separate tax paid to your local city or ward.

- Rate: approximately **10% of previous year's income**
- Billed from **June** each year
- Paid in 4 installments OR deducted from salary monthly

**New arrivals:** You do NOT pay resident tax in your first year in Japan (it's based on previous year income).

---

## Why Taxes Matter for Permanent Residency

Immigration checks your tax payment history when you apply for permanent residency or visa renewal. **Unpaid taxes = major problem.**

Always file on time and keep your tax certificates.

---

## Summary

- Employed at a Japanese company → your employer handles it
- Self-employed or multiple income sources → file Kakutei Shinkoku by March 15
- Keep your withholding slip every January
- Pay resident tax bills that arrive in June
