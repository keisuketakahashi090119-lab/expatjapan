## Exchange Rates: Wise vs Revolut

This is the most important comparison.

### Wise

Wise uses the **true mid-market exchange rate** — the rate you see on Google when you search "USD to JPY." There is no markup on the rate itself. Instead, Wise charges a transparent fee (shown upfront before you confirm).

**Example:** Sending $1,000 USD to Japan
- Mid-market rate: 1 USD = ¥152
- Wise fee: ~$6 (0.6%)
- You receive: ~¥151,088
- True cost: $6

### Revolut

Revolut uses **near-interbank rates** (slightly different from mid-market, but close). On weekdays during market hours, the rate is very competitive. However:
- **Weekend surcharge:** 1% added on weekends when interbank markets are closed
- **Monthly exchange limits:** Standard (free) plan has limits before fees apply

**Example:** Exchanging $1,000 USD to JPY in Revolut (weekday, within limit)
- Near-interbank rate: 1 USD = ¥151.80
- Revolut fee: ¥0 (within free limit)
- You receive: ~¥151,800
- True cost: ~¥200–300 in rate margin (vs true mid-market)

**Verdict:** For a single small exchange, they're similar. For large or frequent transfers, Wise's transparent flat-percentage fee is more predictable.

### Wise

| Transfer Size | Fee (approximate) |
|---|---|
| $100 | $1.50–2.00 |
| $1,000 | $6–10 |
| $5,000 | $25–40 |
| $10,000 | $45–70 |

Fees vary by currency corridor (USD→JPY, GBP→JPY, etc.) but are always shown upfront.

### Revolut

Revolut's transfer fee structure is more complex:
- **Standard plan (free):** Fee-free up to monthly limit; 0.5% after
- **Plus/Premium plans:** Higher limits before fees apply
- **Weekend:** +1% on all exchanges

For large, one-off transfers, Wise is typically cheaper and more transparent.

## ATM Withdrawals in Japan

### Wise ATM Access

- **Free withdrawals:** 2 per month, up to ¥75,000 total/month
- **After free tier:** ¥250 flat fee per withdrawal
- **Works at:** Convenience store ATMs (7-Eleven, FamilyMart), Japan Post Bank ATMs
- **Currency:** Withdraw JPY directly from your JPY balance

### Revolut ATM Access

| Plan | Free Monthly ATM |
|---|---|
| Standard | ¥20,000/month |
| Plus | ¥40,000/month |
| Premium | ¥60,000/month |
| Metal | ¥80,000/month |

After the free limit: 2% fee per withdrawal.

**Verdict:** Wise's free ATM tier is more generous for light users. Revolut's higher-tier plans offer more free monthly withdrawals for frequent cash users.

## Receiving Money in Japan

### Wise

**Wise gives you a Japanese bank account number** — you can receive JPY transfers from Japanese employers, friends, or clients directly into your Wise account. This is a major advantage for freelancers and remote workers.

### Revolut

**Revolut Japan does not provide a Japanese bank account number (IBAN/口座番号)** for receiving domestic JPY payments. You cannot receive a salary or domestic transfer in JPY to Revolut.

**Verdict: Wise wins clearly** for receiving money in Japan.

## Daily Spending: Wise vs Revolut

For everyday spending in Japan:

**Wise card (Visa):**
- Use anywhere Visa is accepted
- Spend from your JPY balance at no extra cost
- No foreign transaction fees
- Works at most Japanese shops and online

**Revolut card (Mastercard):**
- Use anywhere Mastercard is accepted
- Multi-currency spending from your Revolut balance
- Excellent for travel (convert currencies easily)
- Slightly better integration with travel features

**Verdict:** Both work well for daily spending. Revolut has a slight edge for multi-currency travel management; Wise is cleaner for pure JPY daily use.

## Travel Within Asia

If you travel frequently (Japan → Korea, Japan → Thailand, Japan → Taiwan, etc.):

**Revolut advantage:** 
- Holds multiple Asian currencies (KRW, THB, TWD, etc.)
- Competitive exchange rates for currency conversion
- Better travel-focused features (travel insurance on Premium+)

**Wise advantage:**
- More currencies available (40+) with full account features
- More transparent about exact rates

For Asian travel, Revolut has better UX for currency management.

## Japan-Specific Limitations of Both Apps

### Wise Japan Limitations
- Cannot send JPY internationally to some corridors (expanding)
- Some Japanese bank integrations limited

### Revolut Japan Limitations (Important)
- No Japanese bank account number for receiving JPY
- No cryptocurrency trading (unlike EU/US Revolut)
- Fewer premium features than European/US versions
- Weekend currency surcharge

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Wise or Revolut better for Japan?**
Wise is better for international money transfers and receiving JPY in Japan (it provides a Japanese bank account number). Revolut is better for multi-currency travel management and daily spending in multiple Asian currencies. Most expats use both.

**Does Revolut work in Japan?**
Yes, Revolut operates in Japan under a Japan FSA license, but with fewer features than the European/US versions. No cryptocurrency, no Japanese bank account number for receiving payments, and a weekend exchange surcharge apply.

**What is the best app for sending money from Japan?**
Wise is the best app for sending money internationally from Japan. It uses the real mid-market exchange rate with a transparent fee, making it significantly cheaper than bank wire transfers or Revolut for large international transfers.

**Can I use Wise in Japan?**
Yes — Wise works fully in Japan. You get a Japanese bank account number for receiving JPY, a Visa debit card for spending, and can hold and convert 40+ currencies. It's one of the most important financial tools for foreigners in Japan.
