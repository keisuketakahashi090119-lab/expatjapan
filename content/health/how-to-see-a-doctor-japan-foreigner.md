Going to the doctor in Japan for the first time can be confusing. Here's exactly what to do — from finding a clinic to paying the bill.

---

## What to Bring to the Doctor

Always bring:
- **Health insurance card (保険証)** — reduces your cost to 30%
- **My Number Card** — now accepted at most hospitals instead of insurance card
- **Residence card** — for ID if asked
- **Cash** — many small clinics don't accept cards
- **List of any medications you take** (in Japanese if possible)

---

## Step 1 — Find a Clinic or Hospital

**For minor issues (cold, fever, stomach pain):**
Go to a local **clinic (クリニック or 診療所)**. These are small, fast, and cheap.

**For serious issues:**
Go to a **hospital (病院)**. Larger hospitals have specialist departments.

**Finding an English-speaking doctor:**
- **AMDA International Medical Information Center**: 03-5285-8088
- **himawari.metro.tokyo.lg.jp** — Tokyo medical institution search (English)
- Google Maps: search "English doctor near me Japan"

---

## Step 2 — Register at Reception

When you arrive, go to the reception desk (受付).

**Say:**
> "初めてです。保険証を持っています。"
> *Hajimete desu. Hokensho wo motte imasu.*
> "This is my first visit. I have my insurance card."

Hand over your insurance card. You'll fill in a short form about your symptoms.

**Useful words to know:**

| English | Japanese |
|---|---|
| Fever | 熱 (Netsu) |
| Headache | 頭痛 (Zutsuu) |
| Stomachache | 腹痛 (Fukutsuu) |
| Cold / flu | 風邪 (Kaze) |
| Cough | 咳 (Seki) |
| Sore throat | 喉の痛み (Nodo no itami) |
| Allergy | アレルギー (Arerugi) |

---

## Step 3 — See the Doctor

The doctor will ask about your symptoms. Use Google Translate on your phone if needed — most doctors are patient with this.

Bring a translation app: **Google Translate** or **DeepL** works well.

---

## Step 4 — Get Your Prescription

If the doctor prescribes medicine, you'll receive a **prescription slip (処方箋)**.

Take this to any **pharmacy (薬局)** near the clinic. Pharmacies are usually right next door.

**Say at the pharmacy:**
> "処方箋をお願いします。"
> *Shohousen wo onegaishimasu.*
> "I have a prescription."

---

## Step 5 — Pay the Bill

After seeing the doctor, pay at reception. With your insurance card, you pay **30% of the total cost**.

**Typical costs with insurance:**
- Basic clinic visit: ¥500–¥1,500
- With blood test: ¥2,000–¥4,000
- Prescription medicine: ¥200–¥2,000

---

## Emergency: Calling an Ambulance

**Emergency number in Japan: 119**

Ambulances are **free** in Japan. They will take you to the nearest appropriate hospital.

If you need to explain in English: say "ambulance please" — most dispatch operators have some English support.

---

## After-Hours Medical Care

If you're sick outside clinic hours:

- **#7119** — Medical consultation hotline (available in some cities, English limited)
- Search for **夜間救急 (nighttime emergency)** near you on Google Maps
- Large hospitals have 24-hour emergency departments (救急)

---

## Mental Health Resources in English

Finding English mental health support in Japan:

- **TELL Japan (telljp.com)** — English counseling and support line: 03-5774-0992
- **AMDA** — Can refer you to English-speaking psychiatrists
- **JACS (Japan Association of Collegiate Schools)** — Counseling for English speakers

---

## Summary

1. Bring your insurance card every time
2. Go to a local clinic for minor issues
3. Use Google Translate to communicate
4. Pay 30% of the cost with insurance
5. In emergencies call 119
